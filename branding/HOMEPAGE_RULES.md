# Divergify Homepage Rules

Last updated: 2026-03-09
Scope: `/home/jessibelle/Divergify/active/Divergify_Website/index.html`

## Protected copy
- Keep the tagline exactly as written: `For brains that zig when the world zags.`
- Do not replace the wordmark, logo, or tagline without explicit user approval.
- Do not rewrite Field Notes prose when fixing website pages.

## Homepage job
- The homepage is a front door, not a sales page.
- It should help people feel understood quickly.
- It should briefly explain what the site contains.
- It should not try to explain the whole app.
- It should not preview the app in detail.
- It should not make promises the product cannot currently keep.
- It should not use homepage CTA buttons unless the user explicitly asks for them.

## Core audience
- ADHD people
- Autistic people
- People dealing with overload, working-memory issues, masking, and friction in systems built for linear focus

## Voice rules
- Use Divergify's source voice: tech specs, not pathology.
- Blunt, calm, personal, and occasionally funny.
- Funny is allowed. Salesy is not.
- Speak like someone who understands the problem, not like a startup pitch deck.
- No hype phrases such as `revolutionary`, `transform`, `unlock your potential`, `built for different-brain execution`, or similar AI-marketing filler.
- No fake reassurance.
- No toxic positivity.
- No shame framing.
- No fixer language.

## Content rules
- Keep it short.
- Say what the site is for.
- Say what lives here:
  - The Hub
  - Field Notes
  - Divergipedia
  - Dopamine Depot
- Mention Dopamine Depot as the merch arm that helps fund the build.
- Mention Divergipedia as shared language / field manual material.
- Keep legal boundaries factual and brief.
- Do not imply diagnosis, treatment, cure, or clinical outcomes.
- Do not over-explain modes or internal product mechanics on the homepage.

## Structural rules
- Prefer short sections over long blocks.
- Text links are fine.
- Homepage button blocks are not the default.
- Layout changes are allowed if they improve clarity, trust, and accessibility without changing protected copy.

## Research notes
- W3C says users need clear language and a clear page purpose, especially for cognitive accessibility:
  - https://www.w3.org/WAI/WCAG2/supplemental/objectives/o3-clear-content/
  - https://www.w3.org/WAI/fundamentals/accessibility-usability-inclusion/
- Autistica describes accessible online spaces as something users should be able to understand through the "virtual front door." That matches the homepage job here:
  - https://www.autistica.org.uk/news/accessible-online-spaces
- ASAN supports identity-first language for many autistic people. Default to direct, respectful language rather than vague euphemisms:
  - https://autisticadvocacy.org/about-asan/identity-first-language/
- CHADD and Understood both reinforce that ADHD audiences benefit from trustworthy, easy-to-understand information, routines, and step-by-step support:
  - https://chadd.org/
  - https://www.understood.org/en/articles/adhd-fact-sheet
  - https://www.understood.org/en/articles/adhd-mental-load

## Default editing rule
- If the user says `fix the homepage`, default to fixing layout, structure, styling, navigation, metadata, or factual framing.
- Do not rewrite the core message unless the user explicitly asks for copy changes.
